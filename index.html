Thesis

In this section, you will find an overview of my thesis work, which delves into the intricacies of Map-Matching for Train localisation. My research focuses on the concept of Digital Maps, aiming to being able to locate the trai with just Onboard Sensors.

Eventhough my journey through the PhD has just started, the months of extended research have veen fruitful. I have researched extensively the state-of-the-art approaches of map-matching for train localisation, and have managed to write a scientific paper, eventhough this has not been published yet. Hereunder I present the abstract of my recent work:

Precise localisation of rail vehicles is a key element towards the development and deployment of novel train control systems. Localisation of trains can be considered as a 1-D positioning problem, as trains only move on tracks, easing the localisation approach.

Localisation with maps makes a great choice as the maps describe unambiguously train tracks, easing the train localisation and making it an effective choice. On top of that, train localisation with a map requires just onboard sensors making it a low-cost alternative to localisation with track-side equipment, as this equipment makes train positioning costs increase with the reach of the use.

Even though this topic has been widely studied in the literature, there has not been a classification of the used methodologies for map-matching for train positioning. Therefore, the purpose of this paper is to make a classification of the used map-matching algorithms for train localisation. On top of that, this paper also discusses how to build a digital map from a set of coordinates.
